Privacy Policy for DiskOrganizer AI

Effective Date: April 14, 2026

DiskOrganizer AI ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use the DiskOrganizer AI mobile application.

1. Local-First Philosophy

DiskOrganizer AI is designed with a "local-first" approach. This means that:

  • File Metadata: Scanning and analysis of your local storage happen primarily on your device.
  • On-Device AI: When possible, we use on-device models (like Gemini Nano) to categorize files without them ever leaving your phone.

2. Information We Collect

To provide our services, we may access the following:

  • Storage Access: We require permission to read and write to your device's storage to analyze file sizes, types, and locations for organization and cleaning.
  • Google Drive Data: If you choose to connect your Google Drive, we access file metadata (names, sizes, types) to help you organize your cloud storage. We do not download or store your actual file content unless explicitly required for a specific AI task (e.g., summarization).
  • Google Gemini AI: When using advanced "Cloud-AI" features, specific file metadata or document text may be sent to Google Gemini APIs for processing. This data is processed securely and is subject to Google's Generative AI Privacy Terms.

3. Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services to enhance your experience:

  • Google Gemini AI: For intelligent file categorization and document summarization.
  • Google Drive API: For managing cloud storage.
  • Google AdMob: To display non-intrusive advertisements (in the free version). AdMob may collect device identifiers for ad personalization.
  • RevenueCat: To manage premium subscriptions and in-app purchases.

4. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your data. We do not sell your personal information or file metadata to third parties. Your connection to Google Drive is handled via secure OAuth protocols, and we never see or store your Google password.

5. Your Choices and Rights

  • Permissions: You can revoke storage or notification permissions at any time through your device settings.
  • Google Drive: You can disconnect your Google Drive account within the app or through your Google Account security settings.
  • Data Deletion: Since we do not store your file data on our servers, "deleting your data" simply means uninstalling the app or clearing the app cache.

6. Updates to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new policy within the app.
